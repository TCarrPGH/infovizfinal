Abstract

Whooping Cough (pertussis) is a highly contagious airborne virus that causes extreme coughing that can lead to breathing problems. The first whooping cough vaccine was released in the 1940's. However, outbreaks have been on the rise since the mid-eighties. An interactive dashboard containing a bar chart, line-area graph, chloreopeth map, and data table allows users to explore the data from different states and points in time.
